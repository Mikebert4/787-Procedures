# Boeing 787 FCOM Normal Procedures Training Flow

Source: `B787 PKL Flight Crew Operations Manual (FCOM).pdf`, Normal Procedures chapter only. The PDF pages in citations are the file page numbers; FCOM page identifiers are the manual's printed NP section pages. The source manual itself is marked "DO NOT USE FOR FLIGHT"; use this as a training/simulator run-through aid only and defer to your operator/instructor/current approved procedures.

## Operating Frame

- Normal Procedures are intended to confirm that the aircraft condition and flight deck configuration are satisfactory for each phase. They assume normal system operation, full use of automation, and coordination with ground personnel before hydraulic pressurization, flight control movement, or aircraft movement. [PDF p.69; FCOM NP.11.1]
- Before engine start, verify system response using lights/indications. Unexpected messages require dispatch/maintenance consideration. After engine start, EICAS alerts are the primary non-normal indication, and related NNCs apply. [PDF pp.69-70; FCOM NP.11.1-NP.11.2]
- Duties are split preflight/postflight by Captain and First Officer, and in flight by PF and PM. The PF owns taxiing, flight path/airspeed, configuration, and navigation; the PM reads checklists, communicates, performs PF-requested tasks, and monitors. The captain remains the final authority. [PDF pp.70-71; FCOM NP.11.2-NP.11.3]
- CDU entries are made before taxi or when stopped when possible. Before taxi, either pilot may enter CDU/EFB data while the other verifies; in flight, the PM usually enters and the other pilot verifies before execution. [PDF p.71; FCOM NP.11.3]
- AFDS monitoring is continuous: course, vertical path, speed, MCP values, FMA mode changes, and thrust mode changes must be verified. [PDF pp.71-72; FCOM NP.11.3-NP.11.4]

## End-to-End Normal Procedures Flow

### 1. Preliminary Preflight - Captain or First Officer

Reference: [PDF p.77; FCOM NP.21.1]

Prerequisite: Electrical Power Up supplementary procedure complete.

Flow:
- Cycle IRS selectors OFF for 30 seconds, then ON; confirm ON BAT light extinguished.
- Check STATUS display for expected messages only.
- Confirm oxygen pressure, hydraulic quantity, and engine oil quantity are sufficient for flight.
- After crew change or maintenance action, check maintenance documents and emergency equipment.
- Verify fire extinguisher, crash axe, overhead door, emergency descent devices, and other required equipment.
- Set parking brake as needed, especially if brake wear indicators will be checked during exterior inspection.

### 2. CDU/EFB Preflight - Captain and First Officer

References: [PDF pp.78-80; FCOM NP.21.2-NP.21.4]

Timing:
- May begin after Preliminary Preflight.
- Initial Data and Navigation Data must be complete before the flight instrument check.
- Performance Data must be complete before the Before Start Checklist.
- One pilot enters, the other verifies.

Flow:
- IDENT: verify aircraft model, engine rating, and active navigation database date range.
- POS INIT: verify time and enter present position if needed using the most accurate coordinates.
- Route setup: enter route and flight number, activate/execute, select runway/departure or HUD takeoff as applicable, then verify the route on RTE/LEGS.
- Verify or enter departure RNP on the ND/POS REF as required.
- NAV RADIO: tune as needed.
- EFB: verify current databases, review faults/memos/messages, verify documents, initialize flight, and select terminal charts as needed.
- PERF INIT: enter ZFW; reconcile CDU fuel, dispatch paperwork, and EICAS; verify fuel sufficiency and gross weight.
- THRUST LIM: verify OAT.
- OPT/EFB performance: copy/load FMC data as needed, calculate performance, review V-speeds and thrust rating, send output to FMC, then verify uplink on TAKEOFF REF.
- TAKEOFF REF: verify both pages, then accept the takeoff data.

### 3. Exterior Inspection

References: [PDF pp.81-86; FCOM NP.21.5-NP.21.10]

General inspection standard:
- Confirm surfaces/structures are clear, undamaged, complete, and free of leaks.
- Confirm tires, gear struts, engines, doors/access panels, probes/vents/static ports, antennas, and light lenses are satisfactory.

Inspection route:
- Left forward fuselage: probes, sensors, ports, vents, drains, doors, forward outflow valve, pressure relief vents/valves.
- Nose and nose wheel well: radome, diverter strips, forward access door, tires/wheels, gear strut/doors, steering, gear pin as needed, towing lever normal, towing lever pin removed, lights, parking brake indication, wheel well light, APU remote panel.
- Right forward fuselage: probes/sensors/ports/vents/drains, oxygen pressure relief disc, doors/access panels, pressure relief vents.
- Right wing root/pack/lower fuselage: probes/ports/drains, exterior lights, pack/pneumatic doors, CAC inlet/FOD deflector, leading edge devices.
- Right engine/wing/wingtip: access panels, fan/probes/spinner, reverser stowed, exhaust/tailcone, leading/trailing edge surfaces, fuel vent/jettison nozzle, static wicks, lights.
- Right main gear/wheel well: tires/brakes/wheels, chocks as needed, brake wear pins if parking brake set, gear strut/actuators/doors, hydraulic/electrical security, gear pins as needed, wheel well.
- Aft fuselage/tail/left aft: RAT door, outflow valve, probes/ports/drains, stabilizers/elevators/rudder, static wicks, lights, APU exhaust, access doors.
- Left main gear/wing/engine/wing root: mirror the right-side gear, wing, engine, lights, fuel vent/jettison nozzle, leading edge devices, pack/pneumatic/CAC/FOD items.

### 4. Preflight Procedure - First Officer

References: [PDF pp.87-97; FCOM NP.21.11-NP.21.21]

FO overhead/panel flow:
- Flight controls: tail and wings normal; heading reference normal; PFC disconnect guarded and DISC light out.
- Electrical: battery ON; IFE/passenger seats and cabin/utility power ON; APU generator switches ON; generator controls ON; APU selector START then ON as needed.
- Left HUD brightness in; left wiper OFF; towing power lights off; flight deck door power ON; CCR reset guarded; emergency lights guarded; service interphone OFF; passenger oxygen ON light out.
- Window heat: backup and primary ON; INOP lights out.
- RAT unlocked light out; do not actuate RAT.
- Hydraulics: engine primary pumps ON; center electric pumps OFF; electric demand pumps OFF, with expected fault indications before pressurization.
- Seat belt signs and lighting set; landing/taxi/strobe/beacon/runway turnoff lights initially off or as needed per panel.
- Fire panels: APU, cargo, engine fire indications normal; fire switches/arms set correctly.
- Engine: EEC mode normal; start selectors normal.
- Fuel jettison and fuel panels: jettison nozzles off, fuel to remain in, arm off, crossfeed off, pumps off with expected pressure indications, balance off.
- Anti-ice: wing and engine anti-ice selectors AUTO.
- Optional systems: ELT guarded, humidity/cargo temperature controls as installed/needed.
- Air conditioning/pressurization: equipment cooling AUTO, recirc fans ON, temperature controls mid, packs AUTO, trim air ON, ventilation normal, outflow valves AUTO, landing altitude selector in.
- Right wiper OFF; right HUD brightness in; flight director ON.
- EFIS/display setup: minimums, FPV/meters, baro units/setting, airport map range, weather off, traffic/terrain as needed; remember airport map is not primary navigation.
- Oxygen: mask test, normal/100%, pressure sufficient.
- EFB, brightness, air data/attitude source AUTO, PFD/MFD normal.
- After Initial/Navigation CDU data complete and IRS aligned: check flight instruments, expected flags/FMA, MAP mode.
- Landing gear panel set: lever down, alternate gear guarded, autobrake RTO.
- EICAS/MFD/status/checklist display: verify existing engine indications, no exceedance, status messages, and airline database/reset items.
- Radios/audio/flight deck door/engine fire/glideslope inhibit/transponder/printer/lighting/evacuation controls set as needed.
- Adjust seat, rudder pedals, HUD combiner/brightness, and restraints.
- On captain's command, run the Preflight Checklist.

### 5. Preflight Procedure - Captain

References: [PDF pp.98-101; FCOM NP.21.22-NP.21.25]

Captain panel flow:
- EFIS/display setup: minimums, FPV/meters, baro units/setting, airport map, weather off, traffic/terrain as needed, EICAS transfer as desired.
- MCP: flight director ON, autothrottle armed, autopilot disengage bar up, heading/track and vertical speed/FPA references as needed, bank limit AUTO, altitude increment as needed.
- Oxygen: mask test, normal/100%, pressure sufficient.
- EFB/brightness/source/PFD-MFD normal.
- After CDU Initial/Navigation Data complete and IRS aligned: check flight instruments, expected flags/FMA, MAP mode.
- ISFD: approach mode blank, local altimeter set, indications correct, no flags/messages.
- Speedbrake down, reversers down, thrust levers closed.
- Flap lever set to match flap position.
- Parking brake set, with message shown.
- Stabilizer cutout guards closed.
- Fuel control switches CUTOFF and fire warning lights out.
- Alternate flaps panel off.
- Left tuning/control panel, weather radar, transponder, TCAS, VHF, and audio set as needed.
- Adjust seat, pedals, HUD combiner/brightness, and restraints.
- Call for the Preflight Checklist.

### 6. Before Start

References: [PDF pp.102-103; FCOM NP.21.26-NP.21.27]

Start after papers are on board.

Flow:
- Flight deck door closed/locked.
- Confirm CDU/EFB performance data is complete before completing this procedure.
- Set CDU displays: PF normally TAKEOFF REF, PM normally LEGS.
- MCP: V2 set, LNAV armed as needed, VNAV armed, initial heading/track and altitude set.
- Taxi/takeoff briefings complete by the takeoff pilot.
- Verify exterior doors closed.
- Obtain clearance to pressurize hydraulics and start engines.
- If pushback is needed, verify nose gear steering locked out before hydraulic pressurization.
- Hydraulics: electric demand and center pumps AUTO with fault lights extinguished.
- Fuel: left/right pumps ON; center pumps ON if center fuel message shown, noting load-shedding pressure lights until after start.
- Beacon ON.
- Recall/cancel: verify only expected alerts, then cancel messages.
- Trim: set stabilizer for takeoff in green band; rudder trim zero.
- Captain calls and FO runs the Before Start Checklist.

### 7. Pushback or Towing

Reference: [PDF p.104; FCOM NP.21.28]

Flow:
- Establish ground handling communications.
- Do not steer with the tiller or use aircraft brakes to stop during push/tow.
- Set transponder per airport ground tracking capability.
- Set or release parking brake as directed by ground handling personnel.
- When complete, verify tow bar not connected and nose gear steering not locked out.
- Engine start may be done during pushback/towing.

### 8. Engine Start

Reference: [PDF p.105; FCOM NP.21.29]

Flow:
- FO selects secondary engine indications.
- Captain announces start sequence; engines may be started simultaneously.
- Captain calls the engine to start.
- FO selects engine START; captain moves associated fuel control switch to RUN.
- If not starting both together, start the second after the first is stable at idle.
- Autostart handles specified start anomalies. For RR engines, oil pressure monitoring/action is via EICAS and the applicable checklist if an ENG OIL PRESS message appears.

### 9. Before Taxi

Reference: [PDF p.106; FCOM NP.21.30]

Flow:
- APU OFF.
- Engine anti-ice as needed.
- Both pilots verify ground equipment clear.
- Captain calls takeoff flap setting; FO sets flaps.
- Captain checks flight controls slowly and deliberately: full wheel/column travel, return to center, then rudder with tiller disconnect held to avoid nose wheel movement.
- Transponder set per ground tracking capability.
- Secondary engine indications off.
- Recall checked by both pilots; only expected alerts remain.
- Update taxi briefing as needed.
- Captain calls and FO runs Before Taxi Checklist.

### 10. Before Takeoff

Reference: [PDF p.107; FCOM NP.21.31]

Flow:
- Observe engine warmup requirements/recommendations by engine type.
- PF notifies cabin crew to prepare for takeoff; PM verifies cabin secure.
- Takeoff briefing updated as needed.
- Weather radar, VSD, and terrain display set as needed.
- PF calls and PM runs Before Takeoff Checklist.

### 11. Takeoff

References: [PDF pp.108-109; FCOM NP.21.32-NP.21.33]

Flow:
- Before entering runway, verify runway and entry point.
- On entering runway: strobe ON, other lights as needed, transponder TA/RA, brakes released.
- Align with runway and verify heading.
- When cleared: landing lights ON.
- Advance thrust levers to initial stabilization setting by engine type, allow stabilization, then push TO/GA.
- Verify correct takeoff thrust; adjust before 80 knots as needed.
- PM monitors engine instruments and airspeed, calls abnormal indications, then "THRUST SET", "80 KNOTS", V1/automatic V1 verification, and "ROTATE" at VR.
- Captain's hand remains on thrust levers until V1.
- PF rotates toward 15 degrees pitch, follows F/D after liftoff, and establishes positive climb.
- PM verifies positive rate and calls it; PF calls gear up and PM raises gear.
- Above 400 ft RA, PF calls for roll mode as needed; PM selects/verifies.
- Verify VNAV engaged, climb thrust set, acceleration at acceleration height.
- PF calls flap retraction per schedule; PM moves flap lever.
- Engage autopilot above minimum altitude.
- After flap retraction complete, set engine anti-ice AUTO.
- PF calls and PM runs After Takeoff Checklist.

### 12. Takeoff Flap Retraction and Climb/Cruise

Reference: [PDF p.110; FCOM NP.21.34]

Flap retraction schedule:
- Takeoff flaps 20 or 15: at VREF 30 + 20, select flaps 5; then at displayed "5", select 1; at displayed "1", select UP.
- Takeoff flaps 5: at VREF 30 + 40, select flaps 1; then at displayed "1", select UP.

Climb/cruise flow:
- Complete After Takeoff Checklist before climb/cruise procedure.
- At or above 10,000 ft MSL, landing lights OFF.
- Seat belt signs as needed.
- At transition altitude, set/crosscheck standard altimeters.
- If FUEL IN CENTER appears, center pumps ON; when FUEL LOW CENTER appears, center pumps OFF.
- Before top of descent, modify active route for arrival/approach and verify or enter approach RNP.

### 13. Descent

Reference: [PDF p.111; FCOM NP.21.35]

Timing: start before leaving cruise altitude for destination arrival; complete by 10,000 ft MSL.

Flow:
- Both pilots review alert messages and operational notes; PM recalls/reviews.
- Verify/enter VREF on APPROACH REF.
- Set radio/baro minimums as required.
- Set NAV RADIO page for the approach.
- Set autobrake.
- Conduct approach briefing.
- PF calls and PM runs Descent Checklist.

### 14. Approach

Reference: [PDF p.112; FCOM NP.21.36]

Timing: normally starts at transition level and is completed before the IAF, radar vectors to final, or visual approach start.

Flow:
- Do not manually build the selected FMC approach or add waypoints to it; add cold-temperature corrections to altitude constraints when appropriate.
- For IAN with unreliable/inoperative ILS glideslope, select G/S prompt OFF as applicable.
- Seat belt signs as needed.
- At or above 10,000 ft MSL, landing lights ON.
- At transition level, set/crosscheck altimeters.
- Update arrival, approach, RNP, VSD, and approach briefing as needed.
- PF calls and PM runs Approach Checklist.

Flap extension schedule:
- UP at "UP": select 1, command speed "1".
- 1 at "1": select 5, command speed "5".
- 5 at "5": select 20, command speed "20".
- 20: select 25 or 30, command speed VREF25 or VREF30 plus wind additives.

### 15A. Landing - ILS or GLS

Reference: [PDF p.113; FCOM NP.21.37]

Flow:
- PF notifies cabin crew to prepare for landing; PM verifies cabin secure.
- PF calls flaps per extension schedule; PM sets flap lever.
- On localizer intercept heading, verify navaids tuned/identified and deviation pointers shown.
- Arm APP mode.
- Use LNAV, heading/track select, or heading/track hold as needed to intercept final course.
- Verify localizer capture; PM calls glideslope alive.
- At glideslope alive, PF calls gear down and flaps 20; PM lowers gear, sets flaps 20, and arms speedbrake.
- At glideslope capture, PF calls final landing flaps; PM sets flaps and sets missed approach altitude on MCP.
- PF calls and PM runs Landing Checklist.
- At FAF/outer marker, verify crossing altitude.
- PM monitors approach; verify autoland status at 500 ft AGL.

### 15B. Landing - Instrument Approach Using IAN

References: [PDF pp.114-115; FCOM NP.21.38-NP.21.39]

Flow:
- Notify cabin and verify secure.
- Call/set flaps per schedule.
- On localizer/final approach course intercept heading, verify tuned/identified navaids as needed and deviation pointers.
- Arm APP mode.
- Use LNAV/HDG/TRK modes as needed to intercept; verify final course/localizer capture.
- PM calls glide path alive.
- At glide path alive or about 2 NM before FAF, PF calls gear down and flaps 20; PM lowers gear, sets flaps 20, and arms speedbrake.
- At glide path capture, PF calls final landing flaps; PM sets flaps and missed approach altitude.
- PF calls and PM runs Landing Checklist.
- At FAF, verify crossing altitude and crosscheck altimeters.
- PM monitors approach.
- If suitable visual reference exists at DA(H), MDA(H), or MAP, disconnect autopilot as regulations require and maintain glide path to landing.

### 15C. Landing - Instrument Approach Using VNAV

References: [PDF pp.116-117; FCOM NP.21.40-NP.21.41]

Notes:
- Autopilot use is specified for this approach type.
- Procedure is not authorized using QFE.

Flow:
- Notify cabin and verify secure.
- Call/set flaps per schedule.
- Use recommended final approach roll modes: LNAV for RNAV/GPS/VOR/NDB; LNAV or B/CRS for back-course; LNAV or LOC for localizer-type approaches with glideslope off.
- Verify VNAV glide path exists on final approach segment of LEGS page.
- For LOC/BCRS/SDF/LDA final-course intercepts, verify localizer tuned/identified and LOC pointer shown; arm LNAV or LOC.
- Intercept final course with LNAV/HDG/TRK modes as needed, then verify LNAV engaged or localizer captured.
- About 2 NM before FAF and after ALT/VNAV PTH/VNAV ALT annunciation: verify autopilot engaged, set DA(H) or MDA(H) on MCP, select/verify VNAV and speed intervention, then call approaching glide path.
- Approaching glide path: PF calls gear down and flaps 20; PM lowers gear, sets flaps 20, arms speedbrake.
- Beginning final descent, PF calls final landing flaps; PM sets flaps.
- PF calls and PM runs Landing Checklist.
- When at least 300 ft below missed approach altitude, set missed approach altitude on MCP.
- At FAF, verify crossing altitude and crosscheck altimeters.
- PM monitors approach.
- If suitable visual reference exists at DA(H), MDA(H), or MAP, disconnect autopilot as regulations require and maintain glide path to landing.

### 16. Go-Around and Missed Approach Branch

Reference: [PDF p.118; FCOM NP.21.42]

Flow:
- PF simultaneously pushes TO/GA and calls flaps 20; PM sets flaps 20.
- Verify rotation to go-around attitude and thrust increase; adjust thrust as needed.
- PM verifies positive rate and calls it; PF calls gear up and PM raises gear.
- Above 400 ft RA, verify/select roll mode.
- Verify missed approach altitude is set and missed approach route is tracked.
- At acceleration height, set maneuver speed for planned flap setting.
- PF calls flap retraction; PM moves flap lever.
- After retraction to planned setting, select FLCH or VNAV as needed.
- Verify climb thrust and missed approach altitude capture.
- PF calls and PM runs After Takeoff Checklist.

### 17. Landing Roll

Reference: [PDF p.119; FCOM NP.21.43]

Flow:
- PF verifies thrust levers closed and speedbrake up; PM verifies speedbrake up and calls status.
- PM monitors rollout and autobrake.
- After reverse levers are raised, a full-stop landing is required if an engine remains in reverse.
- PF deploys reverse thrust as needed; PM verifies forward thrust levers closed and calls normal or abnormal reverser indications.
- By 60 knots, PF begins moving reverse levers toward reverse idle before taxi speed; PM calls 60 knots.
- After engines reach reverse idle, reverse levers full down.
- Before taxi speed, disarm autobrake and use manual braking as needed.
- Before turning off runway, disconnect autopilot.

### 18. After Landing

Reference: [PDF p.120; FCOM NP.21.44]

Timing: start when clear of active runway.

Flow:
- Observe engine cooldown recommendations: GE at least 3 minutes; RR at least 5 minutes, using no more thrust than normal all-engine taxi.
- Captain positions or verifies speedbrake down.
- APU START then ON as needed.
- Engine anti-ice ON if needed.
- Exterior lights as needed.
- Weather radar off.
- Autobrake OFF.
- Flaps UP.
- Transponder as needed: standby where ground tracking unavailable, transponder where ground tracking is used.

### 19. Shutdown

References: [PDF pp.121-123; FCOM NP.21.45-NP.21.47]

Timing: start after taxi is complete.

Flow:
- Set parking brake and verify message.
- Set electrical power:
  - If using APU, verify APU RUNNING.
  - If using external power, verify available light(s), push external power switch(es), and verify ON.
- Captain sets fuel control switches CUTOFF.
- If towing is needed: establish ground handling communications, verify nose gear steering locked out, and set/release parking brake only as directed. Avoid tiller use and aircraft braking during towing.
- Seatbelts OFF.
- Hydraulics: left/center/right electric demand/center pumps OFF.
- Fuel pumps OFF.
- Beacon OFF.
- Flight directors OFF.
- Transponder STANDBY.
- Select EFB CLOSE FLIGHT.
- FO checks status messages. For PFC self-test after hydraulic shutdown, wait approximately 3 minutes after the hydraulic pressure message before recording status/alert messages in the maintenance log.
- After chocks are in place: captain releases parking brake.
- APU as needed.
- Captain calls and FO runs Shutdown Checklist.

### 20. Secure

Reference: [PDF p.123; FCOM NP.21.47]

Flow:
- IRS selectors OFF.
- Flight deck door power OFF.
- Emergency lights OFF.
- Packs OFF.
- EFB power OFF.
- Both pilots stow HUD combiner and ensure it is fully latched.
- Respect PECS cooldown before removing power; the manual notes a wait of up to 22 minutes may be required and refers to Electrical Power Down in Supplementary Procedures.
- Captain calls and FO runs Secure Checklist.
